Tom Hanks

Academy Award winning actor and filmmaker

Biography

Thomas Jeffrey Hanks is an American actor and filmmaker. Known for both his comedic and dramatic roles, he is one of the most popular and recognizable film stars worldwide, and is regarded as an American cultural icon.

Hanks' films have grossed more than $4.9 billion in North America and more than $9.96 billion worldwide, making him the fourth-highest-grossing actor in North America. He has received numerous awards and accolades, including two consecutive Academy Awards for Best Actor.

Early Life and Career

Hanks was born in Concord, California, on July 9, 1956. He began his acting career in theater and television before gaining recognition for his role in the television series Bosom Buddies. His breakthrough film role came in 1984 with Splash.

Notable Films

  • Forrest Gump (1994) - Academy Award for Best Actor
  • Philadelphia (1993) - Academy Award for Best Actor
  • Cast Away (2000) - Nominated for Best Actor
  • Saving Private Ryan (1998) - Directed by Steven Spielberg
  • Toy Story series (1995-present) - Voice of Woody
  • Apollo 13 (1995) - Astronaut Jim Lovell
  • The Green Mile (1999) - Prison guard Paul Edgecomb

Awards and Recognition

Tom Hanks has won numerous awards, including two consecutive Academy Awards for Best Actor, four Golden Globe Awards, and a Tony Award. He is one of only two actors to win Best Actor Oscars back-to-back.

In 2014, he received a Kennedy Center Honor, and in 2016, he was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Barack Obama.

Personal Life

Hanks has been married to actress Rita Wilson since 1988. He is known for his philanthropy and has been involved in various charitable causes, including space exploration advocacy and veterans' support.

Filmography Highlights

Throughout his career, Hanks has demonstrated remarkable versatility, moving seamlessly between comedy and drama. His performances in films like "Big," "Sleepless in Seattle," and "You've Got Mail" established him as a leading romantic comedy star, while his work in "Philadelphia," "Forrest Gump," and "Saving Private Ryan" showcased his dramatic range.

Legacy

Tom Hanks is widely regarded as one of the greatest actors of his generation. His ability to portray ordinary characters in extraordinary circumstances has made him one of the most beloved figures in American cinema. His work continues to inspire new generations of actors and filmmakers.
